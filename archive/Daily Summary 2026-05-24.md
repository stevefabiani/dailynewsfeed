## 1. Charter Communications confirms breach as ShinyHunters threatens to leak 42 million records

**Category:** Major Breach · **Published:** Sat, 23 May 2026
**Link:** https://cyberinsider.com/charter-communications-confirms-data-breach-as-hackers-threaten-leak-of-42-million-records/
**Source:** CyberInsider

Charter Communications, the U.S. cable and broadband provider behind the Spectrum brand, confirmed it is investigating a cybersecurity incident after the ShinyHunters extortion group listed it on a leak site claiming more than 42 million records of personally identifiable information. The group issued a "final warning" dated May 23 demanding negotiations begin by May 27 or it will release the data. Charter said no sensitive personal information or customer proprietary network information (CPNI) was exfiltrated and that it is cooperating with authorities. The listing extends ShinyHunters' 2026 campaign against Salesforce and enterprise cloud environments that also hit Instructure/Canvas.

## 2. CISA adds actively exploited Drupal Core SQL injection (CVE-2026-9082) to KEV catalog

**Category:** Vulnerabilities · **Published:** Sat, 23 May 2026
**Link:** https://securityaffairs.com/192566/uncategorized/u-s-cisa-adds-a-flaw-in-drupal-core-to-its-known-exploited-vulnerabilities-catalog.html
**Companion:** https://www.tenable.com/blog/cve-2026-9082-highly-critical-sql-injection-vulnerability-in-drupal-core-sa-core-2026-004
**Source:** Security Affairs

CISA added CVE-2026-9082, a critical SQL injection flaw in Drupal Core's database abstraction API affecting PostgreSQL-backed sites, to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog after confirming active exploitation in the wild. The unauthenticated bug affects Drupal 8.0 through 11.3.9 and can enable information disclosure, data modification, and in some configurations remote code execution. Imperva reported more than 15,000 exploitation attempts against roughly 6,000 sites across 65 countries within two days of disclosure, and federal agencies face a mandatory patch deadline. Higher-ed, government, and nonprofit operators running Drupal on PostgreSQL should update immediately.

## 3. Anthropic's Project Glasswing reports 10,000+ critical software vulnerabilities found in one month

**Category:** AI Security · **Published:** Sat, 23 May 2026
**Link:** https://www.anthropic.com/research/glasswing-initial-update
**Source:** Anthropic

Anthropic said partners using its Claude Mythos Preview model under Project Glasswing collectively identified more than 10,000 high- or critical-severity vulnerabilities across systemically important software in roughly a month, including over 6,200 in more than 1,000 open-source projects. Several partners reported bug-discovery gains exceeding tenfold; Cloudflare alone found about 2,000 bugs, 400 of them high or critical. The bottleneck has shifted from finding flaws to verifying, disclosing, and patching them, with some open-source maintainers asking Anthropic to slow disclosures. The trend reshapes vulnerability-management and patch-prioritization planning for healthcare, higher-ed, and government IT teams.

## 6. Instructure strikes deal with ShinyHunters over Canvas hack; NWT government says ~1,700 employees and contractors affected

**Category:** Major Breach (Higher Ed · K-12 · Government) · **Published:** Fri, 22 May 2026
**Link:** https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/canvas-cyberhack-deal-9.7195425
**Companion:** https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/north/canvas-breach-nwt-9.7208039
**Source:** CBC News

Instructure, parent of the Canvas learning management system, confirmed it reached an agreement with the ShinyHunters group claiming responsibility for the April–May breach affecting roughly 9,000 institutions worldwide. The company said it received assurance that no customers would be extorted as a result, but did not disclose whether payment was involved. Separately, the Government of the Northwest Territories disclosed that contact and training records for about 1,700 teachers, government employees, and contractors may have been exposed. Higher-ed, K-12, and public-sector IT teams that use Canvas should review contractual notification obligations and incident-response playbooks.

## 7. Ottawa man charged in Canada and U.S. over Kimwolf botnet operation that infected millions of devices

**Category:** Cybercrime · **Published:** Fri, 22 May 2026
**Link:** https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/ottawa/opp-charges-ottawa-man-following-international-cybercrime-investigation-9.7207909
**Source:** CBC News

Ontario Provincial Police arrested 23-year-old Jacob Butler of Ottawa, who is accused of helping develop and operate the Kimwolf botnet that, with Aisuru, infected millions of IoT devices including IPs on the U.S. Department of Defense Information Network. U.S. prosecutors say they tied Butler to the operation through IP addresses, online accounts, transaction records, and messaging logs obtained via legal process. The case follows the earlier disruption of the Kimwolf DDoS-for-hire service and underscores increasing law-enforcement focus on IoT-based botnets that disrupt government, healthcare, and education networks.

## 9. U.S. tech lobby urges Ottawa to withdraw lawful access bill, citing encryption and trade risks

**Category:** Tech Compliance / Emerging Law · **Published:** Fri, 22 May 2026
**Link:** https://www.cbc.ca/news/politics/lawful-access-bill-pile-on-continues-as-u-s-lobby-group-warns-liberals-of-trade-implications-9.7207808
**Source:** CBC News

The Information Technology Industry Council — whose members include Amazon, Google, and Nvidia — formally called on the Canadian government to pull Bill C-22, arguing the lawful access regime has extraterritorial reach and creates conflict-of-law issues for global providers. Apple, Meta, Signal, and several VPN providers have publicly warned the bill threatens encryption guarantees; CSIS and the RCMP continue to lobby for passage by summer. The dispute is shaping cross-border tech procurement, encryption policy, and compliance planning for any Canadian healthcare, higher-ed, or government entity that relies on U.S. cloud or messaging providers.

## 10. Lawmakers warn U.S. data-broker location-protection rules leave White House, Congress, and CIA exposed

**Category:** Tech Compliance / Audit & Risk · **Published:** Fri, 22 May 2026
**Link:** https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/lawmakers-warn-data-protection-rules-dont-protect-key-sites-including-white-house-and-cia
**Source:** PBS NewsHour

Senators Ron Wyden and Martin Heinrich and Representative Sara Jacobs warned the Trump administration that the April 2025 rules limiting commercial location-data sales to U.S. adversaries omit 736 sensitive locations including the White House, Congress, and CIA headquarters. The lawmakers say data brokers' sale of Americans' location data poses a serious national-security risk when government-employee movements can be mapped by foreign services. They urged a "protection zone" covering all of Washington, D.C. — a development federal contractors and any organization with sensitive personnel should track for compliance impact.
